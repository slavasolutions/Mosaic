Pattern-making is the part of the job that nobody sees. It is also the part that determines whether the pour comes out right.

## Workflow

We start with whatever the client has — a sketch, an existing piece, a measured drawing. From there:

- **Wax cutting** for organic detail and small parts.
- **Clay** for larger forms and any piece that will be cast in sand.
- **3D-printed master** when the geometry is regular and the run is more than ten pieces. We print the master, vulcanise a silicone mould around it, and inject wax for the run.

## Turnaround

A simple pattern: 3-5 working days from drawing sign-off.

A complex pattern (or one needing a printed master): 2-3 weeks.

We are honest about timelines because a missed deadline on a restoration job is usually a missed deadline on a building — and that is somebody else's problem we have just made bigger.
