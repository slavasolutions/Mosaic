A **cold shut** is what happens when two streams of metal meet inside the mould but neither is hot enough to fuse with the other. You get a visible seam — sometimes a hairline, sometimes a crack you can feel.

## What we changed

Three things, in this order:

1. **Gate position.** Moved it off the lip and onto the side of the piece. The metal now travels a shorter distance to fill the detail.
2. **Pour temperature.** Up 20 °C on the C932 recipe. Closer to the chill line, but we lose less to the walls.
3. **Flask preheat.** We were pulling at 200 °C; now 240 °C. Smaller chill on first contact.

The next batch of six poured clean.

## When this fix is the wrong fix

If you keep seeing cold shuts in the same place on different patterns, your gate is wrong, not your temperature. Hotter metal will mask the symptom for a few pours and then stop working.
