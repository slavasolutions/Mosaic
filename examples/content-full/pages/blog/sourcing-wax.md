The wax we use is a roughly 70/30 microcrystalline-and-paraffin blend with a small amount of carnauba for surface. We do not make it. We buy it in 5 kg slabs from a chandler two valleys over.

## What changed this season

The chandler's microcrystalline supplier shifted to a different refinery and the new lot has a noticeably softer set.

| Lot     | Drop point | Penetration | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------------|-------|
| 2024 Q3 | 88 °C      | 14          | Reference batch. |
| 2025 Q4 | 86 °C      | 16          | Slight slump on tall waxes. |
| 2026 Q2 | 84 °C      | 19          | Noticeably softer. Travels poorly. |

The fix is mild — drop the workshop temperature two degrees on pour days, give thin sections an extra night to cool before spruing. The wax still pours fine, it just resists handling on warm afternoons.

## A note on substitutes

A straight paraffin from a different chandler is cheaper and sets harder, but crumbles when you carve detail under 0.5 mm. Worth knowing, not worth buying.
